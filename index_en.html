Hello, I am Chun-Hao Wen, currently an undergraduate student majoring in Mathematics at National Taiwan Normal University (NTNU). Through studying Algebra and Linear Algebra, I developed a profound passion and high sensitivity for pure mathematics. This enthusiasm drove me to self-study Galois Theory over the summer and further explore the expansive field of Homological Algebra.


While delving deep into pure mathematics, Singapore has always been a city I deeply admire. During my first visit in 2018, I was highly impressed by its advanced urban development and spirit of high self-discipline. My second visit in 2025 became a crucial turning point in my life. I had the privilege of engaging in in-depth conversations with local students, whose "extreme efficiency," "drive for excellence," and strict self-demands deeply resonated with my own personal expectations.


During that period, I was honored to receive the "Best Paper Award" at the 2025 Intercollegiate Applied Mathematics Autumn Conference. However, behind this recognition, the future direction of my career brought me immense pressure. After carefully evaluating my personality and the life blueprint I envisioned, I hesitated about continuing solely on the path of pure mathematics. The trip to Singapore acted as a shot in the arm; upon returning to Taiwan, I was even more certain that Singapore is the environment I aspire to be in. Housing Asia's top institutions—the National University of Singapore (NUS) and Nanyang Technological University (NTU)—it possesses profound resources across cutting-edge fields. How could such a perfect utopia not be captivating?


This admiration prompted me to set a clear goal: to gain admission into one of these top-tier institutions for a Master's program in Machine Learning, Data Science, or Artificial Intelligence. To achieve this, I decided to expand my research focus beyond pure algebra, hoping to use these two years to forge a robust, cross-disciplinary background compelling enough for top academic institutions.


Coinciding with the golden era of Artificial Intelligence, the question "How can inanimate machines learn to think?" deeply fascinated me. After taking "Introduction to Scientific Computing" and "Special Topics in Scientific Computing," I mastered a data-centric, strategic problem-solving mindset. Leveraging my strengths in algebra, I approached machine learning from the perspective of linear algebra—starting with Graph Laplacian to solve classification problems, and subsequently integrating Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN) to design an end-to-end investment algorithm for portfolio optimization, officially stepping into the realm of Data Science. Following recognitions from various workshops and academic conferences, my conviction to pursue this path has only strengthened. I aspire to continue translating rigorous mathematical logic into algorithms that solve real-world problems, eventually carving out my own niche in the field of Data Science.

Algebra explores the "structural" nature of mathematics. When writing code or analyzing real-world problems, algebra and linear algebra help me rapidly construct clear mathematical models in my mind. Linear relationships are ubiquitous in real life; especially in computer science, the concepts of matrices and vector spaces serve as the core foundational logic for data processing, graph architectures, and modern machine learning.

Probability and statistics are the sciences of quantifying "uncertainty," providing a rigorous mathematical perspective for ML. They enable me to uncover the underlying distribution patterns of data and accurately assess risks. When facing real-world data full of noise or complex structures, this knowledge allows me to build robust prediction models tailored to various scenarios and establish objective, solid criteria for evaluating model performance by strictly clarifying different types of errors.

Scientific computing is the critical bridge connecting pure mathematical theory with computer implementation. While pursuing theoretical precision, handling inevitable computational errors and limit approximations in practice is crucial. It trained me to build a mindset centered on "computational cost," "algorithm efficiency," and "convergence," applying rigorous mathematical knowledge to provide precise ideas and strict proofs for efficient code that solves real-world problems.

Currently studying and actively researching in this domain.

Functional analysis is a core mathematical branch exploring function spaces and operator theory. To thoroughly understand the foundational logic of Spectral Theory within Graph Laplacian, I initiated a self-study of this subject. By analyzing the properties of infinite-dimensional spaces and operator behaviors, I aim to gain a macroscopic perspective on optimization problems in machine learning and lay a solid theoretical foundation for delving into model generalization.

Factor GCN for End-to-End Portfolio Optimization

2026 | International Conference on Optimization and Machine Learning - Poster Presentation

To overcome the pain points of traditional portfolios being susceptible to "spurious correlations" and the HRP model being limited by historical price noise, this research proposed a Factor GCN model based on graph theory architecture. Through Graph Convolutional Networks, the algorithm deeply fuses long-term fundamental factors with short-term momentum features, effectively filtering market noise, and dynamically outputs optimal asset allocation weights in an end-to-end manner to maximize the return per unit of risk.
